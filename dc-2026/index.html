American History & NMAAHC
10:00 AM
National Museum of American History
✅ Top Priority
Star-Spangled Banner, Lincoln's Top Hat, Dorothy's Ruby Slippers, Entertainment Nation (pop culture), Julia Child's kitchen, America on the Move, First Ladies, Greensboro lunch counter. Free, no timed-entry. Plan ~3 hours.
Enter via Mall entrance (Madison Drive) — puts you on 2nd floor, then head up. Hits all must-sees with zero backtracking.
10:00–10:45
3F WEST Entertainment Nation (45 min)
Pop culture motherlode. Follow the yellow brick road to Dorothy's Ruby Slippers in their climate-controlled spotlight case. Also: C-3PO & R2-D2, original Kermit the Frog, Prince's "Yellow Cloud" guitar (playable replica nearby), Muhammad Ali's robe, Mister Rogers' sweater, Handmaid's Tale costume. Immersive video screens & surround sound. Peek at Lowriding Culture nearby — the Gypsy Rose lowrider is stunning.
10:45–11:15
3F CENTER The American Presidency (30 min)
Lincoln's Top Hat is here. Presidential artifacts spanning 250 years, FDR's fireside chat microphone, campaign memorabilia. Jake will love this.
11:15–11:30
3F CENTER First Ladies (15 min)
Inaugural gowns — iconic and right next to the Presidency exhibit.
11:30–11:45
3F EAST The Price of Freedom (15 min)
Military history from Colonial era to present. The Revolutionary War gunboat Philadelphia — an actual warship from 1776.
11:45–12:15
2F CENTER Star-Spangled Banner (30 min)
The actual flag from Fort McHenry — 30×34 ft, displayed in a dramatic gallery evoking "the dawn's early light." Emotional centerpiece of the museum.
12:15–12:35
2F WEST American Democracy + Greensboro Lunch Counter (20 min)
Jefferson's portable writing desk (Declaration of Independence). The Woolworth's lunch counter from the 1960 sit-ins is in Unity Square nearby.
12:35–1:00
1F Julia Child's Kitchen + America on the Move (25 min)
Julia Child's entire kitchen reassembled here. Then America on the Move — trains, cars, transportation history (Jake's pick). Main gift shop also on 1st floor.
Not open today: "In Pursuit of Life, Liberty & Happiness" (250th anniversary exhibit, opens May 14) and "¡Puro Ritmo!" salsa exhibit (opens April 18).

~2:45 PM
National Museum of African American History & Culture (NMAAHC)
✅ 1:30pm Passes
0.4 mi from American History (walk or 3-min Uber). Passes valid for entry until 4:00pm. Plan ~2.5 hours. 10 stories (mostly underground). Emotionally powerful — mood lifts as you ascend. Sweet Home Café on Concourse level.
Enter via Madison Drive. Security screening → Welcome Center (L1) → escalator down to Concourse → elevator down to C3. The history galleries ascend chronologically from slavery to the present.
2:45–3:00
L1 → C3 Enter, orient, descend (15 min)
Grab a map at Welcome Center. Escalator down to Concourse, then elevator to C3 (deepest level). There may be a short queue for the elevator.
3:00–3:30
C3 Slavery and Freedom (30 min)
The most immersive level. Must-sees: the Point of Pines slave cabin (an actual cabin you walk into), the Thomas Jefferson "paradox of liberty" statue with names of his enslaved people on the wall behind him, and artifacts from the São José slave shipwreck. Move deliberately — physical artifacts hit hardest.
3:30–4:00
C2 Era of Segregation (30 min)
Walk through the segregated railway car. The Emmett Till Memorial is the emotional centerpiece — his original casket in a separate dimly lit room (no photography). He was 14 — close to Jake's age. There's a bench for reflection. Also: the Hagerstown auction block.
4:00–4:20
C1 1968 and Beyond (20 min)
Civil Rights to present. MLK assassination, Oprah Winfrey Show set, Radio Raheem's boombox from Do The Right Thing, Michelle Obama's dress from the 50th Anniversary of the March on Washington. More multimedia, less artifact-dense — move a bit faster.
4:20–4:30
C Contemplative Court (10 min)
Water-filled, light-filled room with overhead oculus — designed for reflection after the intensity below. Don't skip this. Sit for a few minutes. Then grab a quick snack at Sweet Home Café nearby if needed.
4:35–5:10
L4 Culture Galleries — Musical Crossroads (35 min)
After the heaviness below, this floor celebrates achievement and joy. Chuck Berry's red Cadillac El Dorado at the entrance, the P-Funk Mothership, music history from spirituals through jazz, blues, rock, hip-hop. Great energy shift for the whole family.
Skipping (for time): L2 Explore More! gallery, L3 Community Galleries (Sports/Military — unless Jake wants Tuskegee Airmen display, swap 10 min from L4), and "At the Vanguard" HBCU exhibition on Concourse.

For the family: The C3→C2 stretch is emotionally intense but age-appropriate. Katie (8) may have questions. The museum is designed so the mood lifts as you ascend. The Contemplative Court exists for a reason — use it.

Spy Museum & Ford's Theatre
9:00 AM
International Spy Museum
✅ Confirmed
700 L'Enfant Plaza SW. Timed entry confirmed, 9am opening. Plan ~3 hours. Highly interactive — get your Undercover Mission lanyard at the Briefing Center before anything else. Paid admission (not free like Smithsonians). No café inside; L'Enfant Plaza food court next door.
The museum flows as a single narrative path from 5th floor down. No route choices needed — just follow the flow. The Undercover Mission game runs throughout via RFID lanyard. Don't skip the Briefing Center!
9:00–9:15
5F Briefing Center (15 min)
Everyone gets an Undercover Mission lanyard (RFID badge). Choose your cover identity at a kiosk — name, nationality, backstory. Powers the interactive spy game throughout the entire museum. Take the glass elevator up for a great city view.
9:15–9:50
5F Stealing Secrets (35 min)
The gadget motherlode. Hundreds of real spy tools: lipstick pistols, shoe transmitters, buttonhole cameras, hollow coins, Enigma machine. WWII through Cold War to modern cyber. Jake will love this. First Undercover Mission kiosk here.
9:50–10:15
5F Making Sense of Secrets (25 min)
How raw secrets become intelligence. Interactive code-cracking stations, cipher wheels, analyst simulations. Very hands-on — Katie can try breaking codes. NSA "Codes, Ciphers & Mysteries" display is here.
10:15–10:40
5F Covert Action (25 min)
How governments secretly influence events. Sabotage, propaganda, "Anatomy of an Assassination." Dramatic but age-appropriate. More Undercover Mission kiosks.
10:40–11:10
4F Spying That Shaped History + An Uncertain World (30 min)
American Revolution through Cold War to cyber warfare. First-person video accounts by actors playing real spies. Berlin Wall section and spy exchanges are highlights for Jake. Renaissance Venice to Cold War Berlin.
11:10–11:25
4F License to Thrill (15 min)
Spy movies & TV: James Bond, Jason Bourne, The Americans. Real spies comment on which films got it right. Fun, lighter stop. Artifacts from The Avengers and Game of Thrones.
11:25–11:35
4F Debriefing Center (10 min)
Scan your badge for your Undercover Mission performance debrief — spy skills scored and summarized. Kids will compare scores. Full report accessible online later via badge number.
11:35–12:00
3F Camouflage: Designed to Deceive (25 min)
Brand new (opened Mar 1, 2026). Four immersive galleries: Disappear, Distort, Disguise, Deceive. Dazzle ship models, CIA disguise kits, facial recognition interactive. Requires combo or separate ticket ($13 add-on / $15 standalone).
Jake (12): Gadgets in Stealing Secrets and Cold War history will be his favorites. The Undercover Mission game keeps him engaged throughout.
Katie (8): Code-breaking stations, physical gadgets, and Camouflage's visual galleries are great. Museum is reading-heavy — if she zones out on video testimonials, keep moving.
Bring phones: QR-based experiences and photo ops throughout.

2:00 PM
Ford's Theatre
✅ Confirmed
511 10th St NW, 5-min walk from Riggs. Tickets purchased. 4 venues in a half-block campus — plan ~1 hr 45 min. Keep your ticket; you need it at each venue. Acoustiguide ($5) recommended — youth version available for Katie.
Four self-guided venues in order. Ford's Theatre building on one side of 10th Street, Petersen House + Aftermath Exhibits directly across. Verify your tickets include all 4 parts — some ticket types only cover Petersen House + Aftermath.
2:00–2:35
BASEMENT Ford's Theatre Museum (35 min)
Self-guided exhibits on Lincoln's presidency, the Civil War, and Booth's conspiracy. Must-sees: the actual Derringer pistol Booth used and Lincoln's clothes from that night. Interactive elements and 3D figures. Optional Acoustiguide ($5) has youth version — saves reading, adds depth.
2:35–3:00
UPSTAIRS The Theatre (25 min)
The actual theatre, looking almost exactly as it did April 14, 1865. View the President's Box from the balcony — decorated with flag and Washington portrait, just as that night (box interior closed for preservation). NPS ranger narrates the events and takes questions (~15 min talk). This is the highlight for Jake.
3:00–3:15
ACROSS ST Petersen House (15 min)
Cross 10th Street. The boarding house where Lincoln was carried after being shot — the small back bedroom where he died, recreated as it looked that night. Brief but powerful. Show ticket again at entry.
3:15–3:45
3 FLOORS Aftermath Exhibits (30 min)
Enter from Petersen House back porch, work down through 3 floors. The 12-day manhunt for Booth, his capture in a Virginia barn, trial and fate of co-conspirators. Interactive funeral train exhibit. The stunning 34-foot Tower of Books (representing 6,800+ books about Lincoln). Gift shop & Penny Café in the lobby.
Jake (12): Connects directly to Lincoln's Top Hat from American History Museum. Ranger talk in the theatre is the highlight — he can ask questions. Booth manhunt reads like a thriller.
Katie (8): Museum covers assassination, death, and hanging. Ford's notes children under 8 may find it difficult — Katie is at the threshold. The Acoustiguide youth version is age-appropriate and recommended.
"One Destiny" play: 35-min one-act about the assassination (extra $5). Spring 2026 dates TBA — check at box office if running during your window.
